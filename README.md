## Project Overview

This project presents the design and development of a **biomimetic jellyfish-inspired underwater robot**
aimed at achieving low-noise, high-stealth aquatic locomotion.
Unlike conventional propeller-driven underwater robots, this system adopts
a **pulsed jet propulsion mechanism** inspired by the natural swimming behavior of jellyfish.

The robot imitates jellyfish locomotion by periodically expanding and contracting an internal elastic chamber,
expelling water to generate thrust along the body axis.
This biomimetic approach significantly reduces acoustic disturbance,
making the robot suitable for ecological monitoring and close-range underwater observation.

---

## Design Motivation

Traditional underwater robots commonly rely on propellers, which suffer from several drawbacks:

- High noise levels that disturb aquatic life
- Reduced stealth in sensitive environments
- Potential entanglement with plants or debris

Jellyfish, by contrast, achieve propulsion through smooth, rhythmic contraction of their bell structure,
producing efficient thrust with minimal noise.
This project translates the biological propulsion principle into a **mechanically simplified engineering system**.

---

## System Architecture

The robot is designed as a modular mechatronic system composed of:

- **Propulsion Module**  
  A motor-driven mechanism that periodically compresses and releases a flexible water chamber,
  generating pulsed jet thrust.

- **Steering and Direction Control Module**  
  Adjustable flow-guiding fins are placed at the outlet to redirect jet flow,
  enabling directional control without external propellers.

- **Mechanical Structure**  
  A simplified jellyfish-inspired outer geometry improves hydrodynamic compatibility
  while housing internal mechanical components.

- **Control System**  
  A microcontroller-based control architecture coordinates propulsion frequency,
  steering angle, and sensor data acquisition.

---

## Propulsion Principle

The propulsion cycle consists of two phases:

1. **Expansion Phase**  
   The elastic chamber slowly expands, drawing water into the internal cavity.

2. **Contraction Phase**  
   The chamber rapidly contracts, expelling water through the outlet.
   The resulting reaction force propels the robot forward.

By controlling contraction frequency and outlet direction,
the robot achieves both translational and directional motion.

---

## Engineering Features

- Biomimetic pulsed jet propulsion with reduced acoustic signature  
- Mechanically simplified structure inspired by biological motion  
- Modular design for ease of maintenance and future extension  
- Suitable for underwater sensing and observation tasks  

---

## Potential Applications

- Water quality monitoring  
- Close-range aquatic life observation  
- Environmental data collection  
- Educational and biomimetic robotics research

# 仿生水母水下机器人（中文版）
## System Overview

![System Overview](mechanical_design/renders/full_system_overview(2).png)
## 项目简介

本项目设计并实现了一种**仿生水母水下机器人**，
旨在通过模仿水母的自然运动方式，实现**低噪声、高隐蔽性**的水下运动。
与传统螺旋桨推进的水下机器人不同，
该系统采用基于**反冲喷射的脉动推进方式**，有效降低对水生环境的干扰。

机器人通过周期性地压缩与释放内部弹性水囊，
将腔内水体喷出，从而在身体轴向方向产生推进力。

---

## 设计动机

传统水下机器人普遍采用螺旋桨推进方式，存在以下不足：

- 推进噪声较大，容易惊扰鱼群  
- 隐蔽性较差，不适合生态环境观测  
- 容易缠绕水草或漂浮物  

水母则通过收缩外壳挤压内腔体积，
以平稳、连续的方式实现喷水推进，具有良好的静音特性。
本项目将水母的生物运动机理转化为**可实现的机械结构**。

---

## 系统总体结构

系统主要由以下模块组成：

- **推进模块**  
  电机驱动机构周期性挤压水囊，实现反冲推进。

- **转向控制模块**  
  在喷水出口处设置导流结构，通过改变水流方向实现转向。

- **机械结构设计**  
  采用简化的水母仿生外形，兼顾流体适应性与结构集成。

- **控制系统**  
  基于单片机的控制系统，用于协调推进节律、转向控制及传感器数据采集。

---

## 推进机理说明

推进过程由两个阶段组成：

1. **吸水阶段**  
   水囊缓慢扩张，水体被吸入腔体内部。

2. **喷水阶段**  
   水囊快速收缩，将水体喷出，产生反向推力，
   推动机器人沿轴向运动。

通过调节喷水频率与导流结构方向，
可实现前进与转向控制。

---

## 工程特点

- 仿生反冲推进，噪声低、隐蔽性强  
- 机械结构简化，便于制造与维护  
- 模块化设计，支持功能扩展  
- 适用于水下探测与环境监测任务  

---

## 应用前景

- 水质检测  
- 水生生物近距离观测  
- 环境数据采集  
- 仿生机器人与教学演示
