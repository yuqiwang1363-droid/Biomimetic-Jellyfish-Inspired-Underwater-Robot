# Design Background and Motivation

## Background

In recent years, underwater robots have become an important research focus in
marine exploration, environmental monitoring, and biological observation.
However, most conventional underwater robots rely on propeller-based propulsion
systems, which suffer from several inherent limitations:

- High acoustic noise that disturbs aquatic life  
- Reduced stealth in sensitive or ecological environments  
- Risk of entanglement with plants, debris, or marine organisms  

These limitations restrict the applicability of traditional underwater robots
in tasks that require low disturbance and close-range observation.

---

## Biomimetic Inspiration

Biomimetic robotics provides an alternative approach by drawing inspiration
from biological organisms that have evolved efficient and environmentally
adaptive locomotion strategies.

Jellyfish are capable of smooth, low-noise underwater motion through a
**pulsed jet propulsion mechanism**.
Instead of using rotating propellers, jellyfish generate thrust by rhythmically
contracting their bell structure, expelling water from an internal cavity and
producing reaction forces along the body axis.

This natural propulsion method offers several advantages:

- Low noise emission  
- High stealth and minimal environmental disturbance  
- Mechanically simple motion patterns  

---

## Engineering Motivation

The goal of this project is to translate the biological propulsion principle
of jellyfish into a **mechanically realizable underwater robot**.
Rather than replicating the complex biological structure of real jellyfish,
the design focuses on:

- Simplifying biological motion into equivalent mechanical actions  
- Retaining the essential propulsion characteristics  
- Ensuring manufacturability and controllability  

By using a motor-driven mechanism to periodically expand and contract an
elastic water chamber, the robot achieves pulsed jet propulsion in a controlled
and repeatable manner.

---

## Project Objectives

The primary objectives of this project include:

- Designing a low-noise underwater propulsion system inspired by jellyfish locomotion  
- Developing a mechanically simplified biomimetic structure  
- Enabling directional control through flow-guiding mechanisms  
- Integrating propulsion, steering, and control into a modular robotic system  

---

## Application Prospects

The biomimetic jellyfish-inspired underwater robot has potential applications in:

- Water quality monitoring  
- Close-range aquatic life observation  
- Environmental data collection  
- Educational demonstrations and biomimetic robotics research  

The design emphasizes adaptability to aquatic environments and provides a
foundation for future extensions such as sensor integration and autonomous control.

# 设计背景与动机（中文版）

## 研究背景

近年来，水下机器人在海洋探测、环境监测以及水生生物观察等领域
受到了广泛关注。然而，大多数传统水下机器人仍采用螺旋桨推进方式，
其在实际应用中存在一定不足：

- 推进噪声较大，容易惊扰水生生物  
- 隐蔽性较差，不适合生态环境观测任务  
- 螺旋桨结构易被水草或杂物缠绕  

这些问题在低干扰、高隐蔽性的应用场景中尤为突出。

---

## 仿生启发

仿生机器人通过借鉴生物长期进化形成的运动方式，
为工程系统提供了新的设计思路。

水母通过周期性收缩外壳，挤压内部腔体，
以反冲喷射的方式实现水下推进。
该推进方式连续、平稳，且噪声较低，
具有良好的环境适应性。

与旋转式推进方式相比，
水母的喷水推进在隐蔽性和生态友好性方面具有明显优势。

---

## 工程设计动机

本项目旨在将水母的生物推进机理
转化为**可实现、可控制的机械系统**。
在设计过程中，并未完全复制生物体的复杂结构，
而是通过工程简化手段：

- 将生物肌肉收缩等效为机械驱动动作  
- 提取核心推进机理并进行机械实现  
- 兼顾结构可制造性与系统稳定性  

通过电机驱动机构周期性挤压弹性水囊，
实现可控的反冲喷水推进。

---

## 项目设计目标

本项目的主要目标包括：

- 设计一种低噪声的仿生水下推进方式  
- 构建结构简化且稳定可靠的水母仿生机器人  
- 通过导流结构实现运动方向控制  
- 实现推进、转向与控制系统的模块化集成  

---

## 应用前景

仿生水母水下机器人在以下领域具有潜在应用价值：

- 水质检测与环境监测  
- 水生生物近距离观察  
- 环境数据采集  
- 仿生机器人教学与研究  

该设计为后续集成传感器系统和自主控制功能
提供了良好的工程基础。
